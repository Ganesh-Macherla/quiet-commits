So, I wanted to talk about something that has been troubling me since the past few days..Since 2020 has begun, AI tools have become part of every developer’s workflow. 
From code completion to debugging help, tools powered by large language models can dramatically speed up development...to the point where people started relying on AI so much that 
they have stopped using their sense of resoning all together. Now, I;m not saying the use of AI is bad..sure, i use it too, to edit my stuff, write my mails, or even "vibe code" 
a little...more on that later

My point is...AI can be incredibly useful when used the right way. But there’s a difference between using AI as a tool and letting AI do everything for you.
People think its cool to generate a whole project (be it related to tech or even something related to art) JUST using AI...not even putting a single dime's worth of effort.
Sure AI is very convinient for finishing tasks, that said it doesn't hold an ounce of human intelligence called "common sense"...lemme get back to this point.
Artists who spend weeks or even months to create original masterpieces can be geenrated within minutes, coders working for hours to fix errors can be done by AI in seconds.
Technology has always been about making things faster, and AI is simply the newest step in that evolution. 
But speed alone should not replace the effort, thought, and creativity that go into real work.
Again, I would like to iterate that using AI isn't bad...but rather relying AI to fully finish one's actual job is UNFAIR. 
The issue begins when AI is no longer a tool, but a replacement for the work someone is supposed to do.

If someone relies entirely on AI to generate their projects, write their blogs, build their systems, and solve their problems without understanding any of it...
Then the value of the work becomes questionable. I doubt that person presenting the result may not actually understand a single line of code that had been processed.
In creative and technical fields alike, effort and understanding matter.
The point of building something is not just the final output, but the learning, problem-solving, and thinking that happen along the way. Not even me, but to anyone in general who 
has been determined to learn new things through countless number of trial and errors...learning how each system works and understanding the logic behind it through several number of
attempts putting in actual hardwork...all this "AI slop" as we call it has washed us with a sense of utter disgrace. 

Why?? Do we loathe the people who do this? No

Do we hate the way people use something meant to be an assistant as if it were a servant that does all the work for them?? Yes.
