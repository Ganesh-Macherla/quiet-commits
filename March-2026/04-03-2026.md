If common sense were a thing. I bet it still wouldnt have been discovered by the LLM these poeple use. Lemme take up an example, Before modern digital animation pipelines existed, many classic cartoons were drawn frame by frame, by hand. Creating even a few seconds of animation required an enormous amount of time, effort, and skilled artists.
Because of this, studios sometimes reused animation sequences from earlier films. This wasn’t plagiarism ...its a practical technique, still used today known as rotoscoping. The studio already owned the animation, and reusing it saved time while keeping the artistic integrity of the work intact.A famous example is from Disney’s The Jungle Book. The scene where Mowgli sadly throws a rock off a cliff uses animation nearly identical to a moment in The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh where Pooh does the same motion. The animation wasn’t stolen....it was reused by the same studio with full ownership and intent.

That’s proper creative reuse. It becomes plagiarism when someone copies the entire work and presents it as their own. What many people are doing with LLM-generated projects today feels closer to second-hand, indirect plagiarism. The work is generated from massive datasets containing the labor of countless programmers and writers, yet the person presenting the output often contributes little to nothing...when it comes to understanding or putting in original effort. In that sense, they are not reusing their own work like the example we took just now....They are generating results from a system trained on the OWN and ORIGINAL work of others and presenting the outcome as their own creation.
